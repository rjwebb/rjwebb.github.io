Kurt Schwitters and Dada Poetics

Position

This page uses Kurt Schwitters as a focal point for considering Dada poetics through the history of visual and materially organized poetry. The source account places visually oriented poetry among the work of the Dadaists, alongside Rabelais, Lewis Carroll, and the futurists.[^c_07485b_0021]

Visual and spatial organization

Concrete poetry makes space a substantive element of poetic structure rather than treating it as a neutral background.[^c_07485b_0006] It consequently creates a new rhythmic, spatial-temporal reality and rejects traditional linear rhythm.[^c_07485b_0007][^c_07485b_0008] In Joycean terms, this poetic construction emerges from the interaction of the verbal, the visible, and the audible.[^c_07485b_0009]

Read in this context, Dada poetics belongs to a field in which poetic form can be understood as visual, spatial, and materially arranged—not only as a sequence of verbal meanings.
