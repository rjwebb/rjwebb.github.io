Burroughs, Gysin, and the Cut-Up Method

Scope

This page situates the Burroughs–Gysin collaboration and Gysin’s Dreamachine within an experimental tradition concerned with recombination, materiality, and extensions of writing beyond the conventional page. The collaboration and the Dreamachine are treated here as connected examples of the cut-up aesthetic’s visual and perceptual extensions.

Space, materiality, and discontinuity

A related model appears in concrete poetry, which introduces space into the ideogram as a substantive element of poetic structure.【c_07485b_0006】 It thereby creates a new rhythmic, spatial-temporal reality【c_07485b_0007】 and destroys traditional linear rhythm.【c_07485b_0008】 In this account, the poem is not confined to a sequential verbal line but is organized through the interaction of verbal, visible, and audible elements.【c_07485b_0009】

Mallarmé’s Un Coup de Dés is presented as the first poetic work consciously and structurally organized according to space-temporality.【c_07485b_0014】【c_07485b_0015】 Its realization required the use of page space as an element of structure, including words in different typefaces and cases and different reading indications, so that the page became a verbivocovisual score.【c_07485b_0016】【c_07485b_0017】 This provides an important point of comparison for experimental procedures that treat arrangement, interruption, and perception as compositional operations.

Visually oriented poetry also appears in Rabelais, Lewis Carroll, the Dadaists, and the futurists.【c_07485b_0021】 Apollinaire made the first attempt to systematize and theorize the visually figurative poem through his calligrams, although the insufficiency of his procedure is demonstrated by its failure.【c_07485b_0022】【c_07485b_0023】

From text to multisensory event

The same expansion of textual material can be described through Fontana’s poetic work. Contamination permeates that work, whose defining features include the confluence of several artistic disciplines and the intersection of languages.【c_028d86_0001】【c_028d86_0002】【c_028d86_0003】 Fontana aims at poly-dimensionality rather than the mere sum of separate elements.【c_028d86_0004】 Body, gesture, noises, sound, lights, colours, and architectures play inter-linguistic roles in this poetic game.【c_028d86_0005】

Fontana assigns the voice a fundamental structural role: it mediates and catalyzes, while also forming, organizing, constructing, and re-textualizing.【c_028d86_0006】【c_028d86_0007】【c_028d86_0008】 The project is founded on a close interaction between vocalism and writing, producing continuously transforming areas of intervention.【c_028d86_0010】【c_028d86_0011】 Electronics occupies all but negligible space in these areas.【c_028d86_0012】

In this framework, writing produces “pre-texts,” rather than finished texts.【c_028d86_0013】 These pre-texts are places for acoustic and visual transfiguration【c_028d86_0014】 and territories of action that can be reframed beyond the page in spatio-temporal terms.【c_028d86_0015】 They generate writings in motion and poetic performances, which can in turn regenerate writings.【c_028d86_0016】【c_028d86_0017】 The pre-text’s typographical form contains metamorphic germs capable of achieving more complex dynamic structures beyond the page.【c_028d86_0033】

Perception and technological mediation

Fontana’s account also links experimental writing to a changing media environment. The text has taken on an important role in relation to image, sound, and voice, while “new orality” has become more central.【c_028d86_0018】【c_028d86_0019】 The phono-sphere directly influences contemporary poetry, and sound—mediated by modern technologies—can cross time and space and follow one “always and everywhere.”【c_028d86_0020】【c_028d86_0021】【c_028d86_0022】

In Poema Larsen (1983), the microphone is used to produce and articulate sound rather than merely amplify it.【c_028d86_0046】 Sound arises from the relation between voice, gesture, and geometric space, while feedback is caused by reactions that feed the microphones.【c_028d86_0047】【c_028d86_0048】 The microphones pick up phonic units and produce microphonic units.【c_028d86_0049】 The text is limited to the word “microphone,” fragmented into phonematic components that act as a percussor of air masses and set the acoustic space vibrating.【c_028d86_0050】 Each sequence of movements models a sequence of sounds.【c_028d86_0053】

This movement from textual unit to sensory event offers a way to understand the cut-up tradition as more than a procedure for rearranging words. It becomes a broader experimental logic in which fragments, media, space, sound, and perception can be reorganized across different supports. The resulting work may be multidimensional, multidirectional, multivalent, multipotential, polycentric, multilateral, polyrhythmic, and multisounding.【c_028d86_0034】

Related contexts

The spatial and perceptual concerns discussed here connect with Mallarmé and Concrete Poetry, Concrete Poetry: Theory and Method, Visual Poetry and Shape Poems, and Sound Poetry and Performance. Fontana’s account is developed further in Fontana and Epigenetic Poetry.
