Vienna Group

The Vienna Group was the name given to the poets who created early concrete poetry in Austria. [1]

Forms and practices

The Vienna Group experimented with visual and phonetic forms. [2] Gerhard Rühm was a composer, Oswald Wiener was a jazz musician, and Friedrich Achleitner was an architect. [3] [4] [5]

Poetic and theoretical positions

Rühm sought to establish a tension between the graphic and conceptual dimensions of a poem, allowing one dimension to complete the other or to fix a definite aspect of the concept. [6]

Ernst Jandl held that the result of a method used once is a poem identified with the method by which it was made. [7] Heinz Gappmayr considered the connection between notion and sign a principal clarification achieved by concrete poetry. [8]
