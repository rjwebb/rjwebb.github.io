Mallarmé’s spatial-temporal work

Mallarmé’s Un Coup de Dès (1897) is described as his last major work and as the first poetic work consciously and structurally organized according to space-temporality. [c_07485b_0014] [c_07485b_0015]

The problems advanced by Mallarmé’s theory of the “prismatic subdivisions of the Idea” cover the main areas of concern to concrete poetry. [c_07485b_0016] Their realization required the use of page space as an element of poetic structure, together with words in different typefaces and cases and with reading indications; the resulting page functions as a verbivocovisual score. [c_07485b_0017]

Concrete poetry and space

Concrete poetry goes beyond the application of the ideogrammic process as practiced by Ezra Pound. [c_07485b_0005] It introduces space into the ideogram as a substantive element of poetic structure, creates a new rhythmic and spatial-temporal reality, and destroys traditional linear rhythm. [c_07485b_0006] [c_07485b_0007] [c_07485b_0008]

In Joycean terms, concrete poetry results from the interaction of the verbal, “the ineluctable modality of the visible,” and “the ineluctable modality of the audible.” [c_07485b_0009] Joyce does not use the blank space of the page as an element of composition, although his metaphor-words achieve small verbivocovisual ideograms. [c_07485b_0010] [c_07485b_0011]

The architectural comparison

Within the account of concrete poetry, Un Coup de Dès is compared to what Anton Webern represents for electronic music and what Maliévitch or Mondrian represents for concrete painting. [c_07485b_0018] [c_07485b_0019] It is also compared to what Gropius, Mies van der Rohe, or Le Corbusier represents for modern architecture. [c_07485b_0020]

Visual-poetic precedents

Visually oriented poetry is identified in the work of Rabelais and Lewis Carroll, as well as among the Dadaists and futurists. [c_07485b_0021] Apollinaire made the first attempt to systematize and theorize the visually figurative poem through his calligrams, but those calligrams demonstrate the insufficiency of his procedure through their failure. [c_07485b_0022] [c_07485b_0023]
