Noigandres Group and Brazilian Concrete Poetry

The theoretical foundations of concrete poetry, as articulated by one of its Brazilian practitioners, were laid out in Décio Pignatari's article "Concrete Poetry: A Brief Structural-Historical Guideline," originally published in ad–arquitetura e decoração, São Paulo, ano IV, n. 2, March–April 1957.[c_07485b_0001]

Theoretical Precursors

Mallarmé

Within this framework, Stéphane Mallarmé's Un Coup de Dès (1897) is identified as his last, impressive work.[c_07485b_0014] It is described as the first poetic work consciously and structurally organized according to space-temporality.[c_07485b_0015] The problems advanced by Mallarmé's theory of "prismatic subdivisions of the Idea" cover the main areas of concern to concrete poetry.[c_07485b_0016] Realizing those problems required using page space as an element of structure — deploying words in different typefaces, cases, and reading indications — so that the page becomes a verbivocovisual score.[c_07485b_0017] In the context of concrete poetry's intellectual genealogy, Un Coup de Dès occupies a role analogous to that of Anton Webern for electronic music,[c_07485b_0018] to that of Maliévitch or Mondrian for concrete painting,[c_07485b_0019] and to that of Gropius, Mies van der Rohe, or Le Corbusier for modern architecture.[c_07485b_0020] (See also Mallarmé and Concrete Poetry.)

Ezra Pound and the Ideogram

Ezra Pound gave an elementary but clear idea of the ideogram based on Ernest Fenollosa's studies of Chinese writing.[c_07485b_0002] The Chinese define the color red with abbreviated signs for rose, rust, cherry, and flamingo;[c_07485b_0003] according to Pound, the Chinese ideogram for red is based on things everyone knows.[c_07485b_0004] Concrete poetry, however, goes beyond the application of the ideogrammic process as practiced by Pound,[c_07485b_0005] introducing space into the ideogram as a substantive element of poetic structure[c_07485b_0006] and thereby creating a new rhythmic, spatial-temporal reality.[c_07485b_0007] In doing so, it destroys traditional linear rhythm.[c_07485b_0008]

James Joyce

In Joycean terms, concrete poetry results from the interaction of the verbal, the ineluctable modality of the visible, and the ineluctable modality of the audible.[c_07485b_0009] Joyce achieves a small verbivocovisual ideogram in each of his metaphorwords — for example, "silvamoonlake" — which plays on the Latin silva (forest) and the orthographic-phonetic representation of "silver-silva."[c_07485b_0011][c_07485b_0012] This example illustrates the "panaroma of all flores of speech" that Joyce began in Ulysses and realized completely in Finnegans Wake.[c_07485b_0013] However, Joyce does not use the blank space of the page as an element of composition.[c_07485b_0010]

Apollinaire and Visually Oriented Poetry

Visually oriented poetry can be found across a range of antecedents, including Rabelais, Lewis Carroll, the Dadaists, and the futurists.[c_07485b_0021] The first attempt to systematize and theorize on the visually figurative poem was made by Apollinaire with his calligrams.[c_07485b_0022] Those calligrams, however, demonstrate in their failure the insufficiency of his procedure.[c_07485b_0023] Apollinaire also used the pseudonym Gabriel Arboin.[c_07485b_0024]

Fontana and Epigenetic Poetry

Related developments in experimental poetics involved the Italian poet Fontana, whose work is distinguished by the confluences of several artistic disciplines[c_028d86_0002] and by the intersections of languages.[c_028d86_0003] Contamination always permeates Fontana's poetic work,[c_028d86_0001] and the work aims at poly-dimensionality rather than the mere sum of its elements.[c_028d86_0004] In Fontana's poetic game, body, gesture, noises, sound, lights, colours, and architectures play inter-linguistic roles.[c_028d86_0005]

Fontana assigns a fundamental structural role to the voice,[c_028d86_0006] which mediates and catalyzes,[c_028d86_0007] and which forms, organizes, constructs, and re-textualizes.[c_028d86_0008] Fontana's poetic project has roots in experiments of sound poetry that began in the late 1960s[c_028d86_0009] and is founded on a close interaction between vocalism and writing.[c_028d86_0010] In this project, vocalism and writing interact with each other, creating continuously transforming areas of intervention,[c_028d86_0011] and electronics occupies all but negligible spaces in these areas.[c_028d86_0012]

Fontana's writing produces pre-texts rather than texts.[c_028d86_0013] Pre-texts are places to transfigure acoustically and visually,[c_028d86_0014] territories of action to re-frame beyond the page in spatio-temporal terms,[c_028d86_0015] and they generate writings in motion and poetic performances[c_028d86_0016] that are capable of re-generating further writings.[c_028d86_0017] Since the beginning of the 1980s, Fontana has been sustaining new functions in writing that lead to the conception of text as integrated text, polytext in resonance, multi-poetic sonorous hyper-text, and transversal ultra-text based on action languages.[c_028d86_0032]

In the work Poema Larsen (1983), the use of the microphone aims at producing and articulating sound, not merely amplifying it,[c_028d86_0046] and the sound is the fruit of a relation between voice, gesture, and geometric space.[c_028d86_0047] The text in Poema Larsen is limited to the word "microphone," fragmented in its phonematic components, and acts as a percussor of masses of air to put in vibration the acoustic space.[c_028d86_0050] The only word used is the pretext to trigger the Larsen effect, which is modulated by the action,[c_028d86_0052] and each sequence of movements models a sequence of sounds.[c_028d86_0053]

The poet in this paradigm transforms into a poly-artist who takes possession of electronic, videographic, cinema, photography, sonorous universe, theatrical dimension, and rhythmic universe,[c_028d86_0035] acting poetically using all techniques, supports, and spaces, without renouncing body, gesture, and voice, which constitute the basis of a new poetic attitude.[c_028d86_0036] The poly-artist is the creator and actor of Hyperpoetry,[c_028d86_0042] and the poetic body undergoes subsequent processes of reorganization and progressive plastic modeling — a condition that may be described as Epigenetic Poetry.[c_028d86_0043]
