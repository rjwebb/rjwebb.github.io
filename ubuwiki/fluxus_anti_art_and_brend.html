Fluxus, Anti-Art, and Brend

Spatial and verbivocovisual poetry

Concrete poetry introduces space into the ideogram as a substantive element of poetic structure and creates a new rhythmic, spatial-temporal reality. It destroys traditional linear rhythm and results from the interaction of the verbal, the visible, and the audible.12345

Mallarmé’s Un Coup de Dès (1897) is described as the first poetic work consciously and structurally organized according to space-temporality.67 The problems advanced by Mallarmé’s theory of the “prismatic subdivisions of the Idea” cover the main areas of concern to concrete poetry.8 Their realization required the use of page space as an element of structure, with words in different typefaces and cases and with reading indications, making the page a verbivocovisual score.9

Visually oriented poetry can be found in Rabelais, Lewis Carroll, the Dadaists, and futurists.10 Apollinaire made the first attempt to systematize and theorize the visually figurative poem through his calligrams, although the calligrams demonstrate the insufficiency of his procedure.1112

Sound, writing, and electronic mediation

Fontana’s poetic work is distinguished by the confluence of several artistic disciplines and by intersections among languages.1314 It aims at poly-dimensionality rather than the mere sum of its elements.15 Body, gesture, noises, sound, lights, colours, and architectures play inter-linguistic roles in this poetic game.16

Fontana assigns the voice a fundamental structural role: the voice mediates and catalyzes, and it forms, organizes, constructs, and re-textualizes.171819 The project has roots in sound-poetry experiments that began in the late 1960s and is founded on a close interaction between vocalism and writing.2021 These elements create continuously transforming areas of intervention, in which electronics occupies all but negligible spaces.2223

Fontana’s writing produces pre-texts rather than texts.24 These pre-texts are places for acoustic and visual transfiguration and territories of action that can be reframed beyond the page in spatio-temporal terms.2526 They generate writings in motion and poetic performances capable of regenerating writings.2728

The sound dimension strongly influences writing in the technical ambit, while the orality of this context is based on artificial memories of writing and on electronics rather than natural memory.2930 Texts commonly recuperate rhythmic dimensions from twentieth-century experimental techniques, street sounds, mass-media scansions, and popular or archaic forms.31 Text structures intended as scores, which began with difficulty at the start of the twentieth century, assume more significant roles.32

Poema Larsen

In Poema Larsen (1983), the microphone is used to produce and articulate sound, not merely to amplify it.33 The sound results from a relation among voice, gesture, and geometric space.34 The action produces feedback modulations through reactions that feed the microphones, which pick up phonic units and produce microphonic units.3536

The text of Poema Larsen is limited to the word “microphone,” fragmented into phonematic components; it acts as a percussor of air masses, setting the acoustic space into vibration.37 Interference generates sounds under the control of modulating gesture.38 The single word serves as the pretext for triggering the Larsen effect, which is modulated by the action, and each sequence of movements models a sequence of sounds.3940

Fontana’s voice in motion, sustained and mediated by modern technologies, produces sounds and draws spaces, becoming text and ultra-text, body and ultra-body, animus and anima, or a “hypervox.”41 Modern technologies can highlight imperceptible bodily sounds, amplify innermost flatus, and generate new vocal universes through sophisticated software.42 For the hyper-poetic form Epigenetic Poetry, sound may be articulated behind an “electrophonic mask” or “digital form,” with sound functioning as a fundamental aspect of language.43
