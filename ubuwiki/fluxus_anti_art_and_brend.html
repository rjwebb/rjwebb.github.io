Fluxus, Anti-Art, and Brend

Fluxus, Anti-Art, and Brend is an encyclopedia subject treated here through experimental practices in concrete, sound, performance, and electronically mediated poetry.

Concrete poetry and spatial structure

Concrete poetry introduces space into the ideogram as a substantive element of poetic structure, creates a new rhythmic and spatial-temporal reality, and destroys traditional linear rhythm.123 In Joycean terms, it results from interaction among the verbal, the visible, and the audible.4

Mallarmé and Concrete Poetry is associated with Un Coup de Dés, which is described as Mallarmé’s last impressive work and as the first poetic work consciously and structurally organized according to space-temporality.56 Mallarmé’s theory of the “prismatic subdivisions of the Idea” addresses problems that cover the main areas of concern to concrete poetry.7 Their realization required page space to function as an element of structure, with words in different typefaces and cases and with reading indications; the page thereby becomes a verbivocovisual score.8

Visually oriented poetry is found in Rabelais, Lewis Carroll, the Dadaists, and futurists.9 Apollinaire made the first attempt to systematize and theorize the visually figurative poem through his calligrams, although the calligrams also demonstrate the insufficiency of that procedure.1011

Voice, writing, and electronic mediation

The poetic work of Fontana and Epigenetic Poetry is distinguished by the confluence of several artistic disciplines and by intersections among languages.1213 It aims at poly-dimensionality rather than the mere sum of its elements.14 Body, gesture, noises, sound, lights, colours, and architectures have inter-linguistic roles in this poetic practice.15

Fontana assigns the voice a fundamental structural role.16 The voice mediates and catalyzes, and forms, organizes, constructs, and re-textualizes.1718 The project has roots in sound poetry experiments that began in the late 1960s and is founded on a close interaction between vocalism and writing.1920 These elements create continuously transforming areas of intervention in which electronics occupies all but negligible spaces.2122

Fontana’s writing produces pre-texts rather than texts.23 Pre-texts are places for acoustic and visual transfiguration and territories of action that can be reframed beyond the page in spatio-temporal terms.2425 They generate writings in motion and poetic performances capable of regenerating writings.2627

The sound dimension strongly influences writing in the technical ambit, while orality in this context is based on artificial memories of writing and on electronics rather than natural memory.2829 Texts commonly recuperate rhythmic dimensions from twentieth-century experimental techniques, street sounds, mass-media scansions, and popular or archaic forms.30 Text structures intended as scores, which began with difficulty at the start of the twentieth century, assume more significant roles.31

Poema Larsen

In Poema Larsen (1983), the microphone produces and articulates sound rather than merely amplifying it.32 The sound results from a relation among voice, gesture, and geometric space.33 The action produces feedback modulations through reactions that feed the microphones, which pick up phonic units and produce microphonic units.3435

The text is limited to the word “microphone,” fragmented into phonematic components. It acts as a percussor of air masses, setting the acoustic space into vibration.36 Interference generates sounds under the control of modulating gesture.37 The single word serves as the pretext for triggering the Larsen effect, which is modulated by the action, and each sequence of movements models a sequence of sounds.3839

Fontana’s voice in motion, sustained and mediated by modern technologies, produces sounds and draws spaces, becoming text and ultra-text, body and ultra-body, animus and anima, or a “hypervox.”40 Modern technologies can highlight imperceptible bodily sounds, amplify innermost flatus, and generate new vocal universes through sophisticated software.41 In the hyper-poetic form Epigenetic Poetry, sound can be articulated behind an “electrophonic mask” or “digital form,” functioning as a fundamental aspect of language.42
