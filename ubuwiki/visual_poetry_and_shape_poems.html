Visual structure

Visually oriented poetry appears in the work of Rabelais, Lewis Carroll, the Dadaists, and the futurists. [c_07485b_0021] The first attempt to systematize and theorize the visually figurative poem was made by Apollinaire through his calligrams; the same account describes the calligrams’ failure as demonstrating the insufficiency of that procedure. [c_07485b_0022] [c_07485b_0023]

Concrete poetry extends the ideogrammic process beyond its use by Ezra Pound. [c_07485b_0005] It introduces space into the ideogram as a substantive element of poetic structure and creates a new rhythmic, spatial-temporal reality. [c_07485b_0006] [c_07485b_0007] In doing so, it destroys traditional linear rhythm. [c_07485b_0008] Its operation is described in Joycean terms as an interaction among the verbal, the visible, and the audible. [c_07485b_0009]

Mallarmé’s Un Coup de Dès (1897) is presented as the first poetic work consciously and structurally organized according to space-temporality. [c_07485b_0014] [c_07485b_0015] Realizing its problems required using the page as an element of structure, with words in different typefaces and cases and with reading indications, so that the page became a verbivocovisual score. [c_07485b_0016] [c_07485b_0017]

From the page to multidimensional work

Fontana’s poetic work is distinguished by the confluence of several artistic disciplines and by intersections among languages. [c_028d86_0002] [c_028d86_0003] Contamination permeates that work, whose aim is poly-dimensionality rather than the mere sum of its elements. [c_028d86_0001] [c_028d86_0004] In Fontana’s poetic game, body, gesture, noises, sound, lights, colours, and architectures play inter-linguistic roles. [c_028d86_0005]

Fontana’s writing produces pre-texts rather than texts. [c_028d86_0013] These pre-texts are places for acoustic and visual transfiguration and territories of action that can be reframed beyond the page in spatio-temporal terms. [c_028d86_0014] [c_028d86_0015] They generate writings in motion and poetic performances, which can in turn regenerate writings. [c_028d86_0016] [c_028d86_0017] In typographical form, the pre-text contains metamorphic germs capable of achieving the complexity of subsequent dynamic structures beyond the page. [c_028d86_0033]

Poema Larsen

In Poema Larsen (1983), the microphone is used to produce and articulate sound, rather than merely to amplify it. [c_028d86_0046] The sound results from a relation among voice, gesture, and geometric space. [c_028d86_0047] The action produces modulations of feedback through reactions that feed the microphones, while the microphones pick up phonic units and produce microphonic units. [c_028d86_0048] [c_028d86_0049]

The text is limited to the word “microphone,” fragmented into phonematic components; it acts as a percussor of masses of air, setting the acoustic space into vibration. [c_028d86_0050] Interference generates sounds and is controlled by modulating gesture. [c_028d86_0051] The single word functions as a pretext for triggering the Larsen effect, which is modulated by the action. [c_028d86_0052] Each sequence of movements models a sequence of sounds. [c_028d86_0053]
