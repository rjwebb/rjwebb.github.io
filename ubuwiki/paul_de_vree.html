Paul de Vree

Paul de Vree was a Belgian poet associated with sound poetry and performance and concrete poetry.

Career and activities

De Vree organised the 1962 Antwerp Exposition of Objective Poetry and met the poet and composer Henri Chopin. His work developed in relation to the international concrete poetry movement, while maintaining a distinct interest in the phonetic and sonic dimensions of poetry.

Poetic theory

De Vree regarded poetry as a phonetic phenomenon. He distinguished his approach from French sound poetry, and argued that purely visual verbal poetry does not exist. His position connected the material appearance of writing with its acoustic and vocal potential, in a manner related to broader debates about language, writing, and the materiality of language.

Poems

Among De Vree’s cited works are “Vertigo Gli,” including its sonorisation, and “A rose.” His work has also been associated with the emergence of electronic poetry.
