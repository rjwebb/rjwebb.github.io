Fontana and Epigenetic Poetry

Poetics of contamination and poly-dimensionality

Contamination always permeates Fontana’s poetic work. Confluences of several artistic disciplines and intersections of languages distinguish it. Fontana aims at poly-dimensionality rather than at the mere sum of separate elements. In this poetic game, body, gesture, noises, sound, lights, colours, and architectures play inter-linguistic roles. [c_028d86_0001] [c_028d86_0002] [c_028d86_0003] [c_028d86_0004] [c_028d86_0005]

Fontana assigns the voice a fundamental structural role. The voice mediates and catalyzes; it forms, organizes, constructs, and re-textualizes. [c_028d86_0006] [c_028d86_0007] [c_028d86_0008] The project has roots in experiments in sound poetry that began in the late 1960s and is founded on a close interaction between vocalism and writing. [c_028d86_0009] [c_028d86_0010] Vocalism and writing interact to create continuously transforming areas of intervention, in which electronics occupies all but negligible spaces. [c_028d86_0011] [c_028d86_0012]

Pre-text and dynamic writing

Fontana’s writing produces pre-texts rather than texts. [c_028d86_0013] Pre-texts are places for acoustic and visual transfiguration and territories of action to be reframed beyond the page in spatio-temporal terms. [c_028d86_0014] [c_028d86_0015] They generate writings in motion and poetic performances capable of regenerating writings. [c_028d86_0016] [c_028d86_0017]

In its typographical form, the pre-text contains metamorphic germs capable of achieving the complexity of subsequent dynamic structures—“hyperhyphos”—beyond the page. [c_028d86_0033] It permits new connections with textual forms in the performative intermedial ambit, rendering the work multidimensional and multidirectional, multivalent and multipotential, polycentric and multilateral, polyrhythmic and multisounding. [c_028d86_0034]

Since the beginning of the 1980s, Fontana has sustained new functions in writing leading to the conception of text as integrated text, polytext in resonance, multi-poetic sonorous hyper-text, and transversal ultra-text based on action languages. [c_028d86_0032] The poetry creates tensions through contamination of systems, penetration of separate universes, and use of new media and supports. [c_028d86_0038] It conjugates scientific energies with the energies of memory and body through a different conception of the materiality of language. [c_028d86_0039]

Voice, electronics, and new orality

In the years immediately preceding the source’s publication, text played an important role with image, sound, and voice, while the role of “new orality” was becoming more central. [c_028d86_0018] [c_028d86_0019] The phono-sphere directly influences contemporary poetry. [c_028d86_0020] Sound and voice are fluid and can easily cross time and space; mediated by modern technologies, sound can follow one always and everywhere. [c_028d86_0021] [c_028d86_0022]

Vocalism has to undergo writing that is already in the phase of the poetic project, and a renewed function must be assigned to the voice in the literary ambit. [c_028d86_0023] [c_028d86_0024] In the technical ambit, the sound dimension strongly influences writing, rather than vocalism merely adhering to text. [c_028d86_0025] Orality in this context is based on artificial memories of writing and on electronics, not on natural memory. [c_028d86_0026]

The media panorama has greatly conditioned text elaboration. [c_028d86_0027] Texts commonly recuperate rhythmic dimensions from twentieth-century experimental techniques, street sounds, mass-media scansions, and popular or archaic ways. [c_028d86_0028] Text structures intended as scores, which began with difficulty at the beginning of the twentieth century, now assume more significant roles. [c_028d86_0029] In hypermedia society, the voice and vocal text play central roles in poetry and prose. [c_028d86_0030] Confluences and digressions, conditioned and supported by vocal imprinting, might distinguish ultra-textual directions. [c_028d86_0031]

The voice reconnects to a long-lost orality. [c_028d86_0040] Through new tools of sound synthesis, it also places itself in the position of an unheeded vocality. [c_028d86_0041]

The poly-artist and Epigenetic Poetry

The poet transforms into a poly-artist who takes possession of electronic, videographic, cinematic, photographic, sonorous, theatrical, and rhythmic universes. [c_028d86_0035] The poly-artist acts poetically through all techniques, supports, and spaces without renouncing body, gesture, and voice, which form the basis of a new poetic attitude. [c_028d86_0036] This poly-artist widens and enervates the boundaries of poetry. [c_028d86_0037] The polyartist is the creator and actor of Hyperpoetry. [c_028d86_0042]

The poetic body undergoes subsequent processes of reorganization and progressive plastic modeling, so that one may speak of Epigenetic poetry. [c_028d86_0043] The artist’s technological sensibility is revealed in the way the artist relates to the means, and the more specificity is exalted, the more the range of technological signifiers supporting the work is enriched. [c_028d86_0044] [c_028d86_0045]

Poema Larsen

In Poema Larsen (1983), the microphone is used to produce and articulate sound, not merely to amplify it. [c_028d86_0046] The sound results from a relation between voice, gesture, and geometric space. [c_028d86_0047] The action gives rise to feedback modulations caused by reactions that feed the microphones. [c_028d86_0048] The microphones pick up phonic units and produce microphonic units. [c_028d86_0049]

The text is limited to the word “microphone,” fragmented into its phonematic components. It acts as a percussor of masses of air, putting the acoustic space into vibration. [c_028d86_0050] Interference generates sounds controlled by modulating gesture. [c_028d86_0051] The single word is a pretext for triggering the Larsen effect, which the action modulates. [c_028d86_0052] Each sequence of movements models a sequence of sounds. [c_028d86_0053]

Hypervox and the electrophonic mask

Fontana’s voice in motion, sustained and mediated by modern technologies, produces sounds and draws spaces, becoming text and ultra-text, body and ultra-body, animus and anima: a hypervox. [c_028d86_0054] Modern technologies highlight imperceptible sounds of the body, amplify innermost flatus, and generate new vocal universes through sophisticated software, up to the total devastation of initial acoustic diagrams. [c_028d86_0055]

One can speak of an “electrophonic mask,” or “digital form,” behind which sound is articulated as a fundamental aspect of language in the hyper-poetic form of Epigenetic Poetry. [c_028d86_0056]
