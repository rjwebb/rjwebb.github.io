Abraham Lincoln Gillespie and Syntactic Revolution

This page uses “syntactic revolution” as a provisional interpretive frame for the syntactic experimentation attributed to Abraham Lincoln Gillespie in the plan rationale.

Gillespie is presented here as an under-recognised figure of experimental language. The rationale identifies his dates as 1895–1950 and describes his language as idiosyncratic. It also places his work in transition magazine alongside Joyce.

The page therefore treats Gillespie’s importance as lying in the relation between his idiosyncratic language and his syntactic experimentation. This places his work within the broader history of experimental writing documented across the wiki, while leaving the detailed account of his methods to Sol J. Leon’s biographical source.
